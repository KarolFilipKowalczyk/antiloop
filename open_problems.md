# Open Problems

Three problems for the current paper.

---

**O1. General case.** The anti-loop theorem is proved for worst-case inputs and for input-independent transition functions under stochastic inputs. The general case — arbitrary transition functions with input-dependent routing — is conjectured but unproved. The gap: can an input-dependent δ beat the rotating δ in expected loop time?

**O2. Scale testing.** CSN analysis at 10⁴–10⁵ nodes. Full Broido-Clauset protocol. Current results (1000 nodes, 10 seeds) are stable but moderate scale.

**O3. Encoding uniqueness.** The theorem proves flat encoding fails. Does it uniquely favor binary comparison trees, or do other logarithmic-access encodings (DAGs, bounded-width circuits) survive equally?
